Domain 1: Planning and Preparation

This domain highlights how teachers plan for student learning, demonstrate content knowledge, and design effective instruction. Below are key components principals look for — you can personalize each section with your own reflections, evidence, or examples.

1a: Demonstrating Knowledge of Content and Pedagogy

What principals look for: Clear understanding of subject matter, grade-level standards, and teaching methods.

Your Example: Describe a lesson where your deep understanding of the content helped students connect ideas.

1b: Demonstrating Knowledge of Students

What principals look for: Awareness of student backgrounds, learning styles, interests, and needs.

Your Example: Describe how you differentiate instruction based on your students’ needs or interests.

1c: Setting Instructional Outcomes

What principals look for: Clear, measurable learning goals aligned with standards and appropriate for all learners.

Your Example: Include an example of how you create learning objectives or success criteria.

1d: Demonstrating Knowledge of Resources

What principals look for: Effective use of school, community, and digital resources to support instruction.

Your Example: List resources or tools you’ve used to enhance a lesson (websites, technology, community guests, etc.).

1e: Designing Coherent Instruction

What principals look for: Lessons that build on prior knowledge, use engaging materials, and follow a logical progression.

Your Example: Describe a well-sequenced lesson plan or unit you’ve created.

1f: Designing Student Assessments

What principals look for: Assessments aligned to outcomes that measure understanding and guide instruction.

Your Example: Include how you use formative or summative assessments to adjust your teaching.
