Domain 4: Professional Responsibilities

This domain focuses on the responsibilities that extend beyond the classroom — reflection, communication, collaboration, and professional growth. Principals look for teachers who demonstrate commitment to their students, colleagues, and continuous improvement.

4a: Reflecting on Teaching

What principals look for: Honest reflection, identification of strengths and areas for growth, and adjustments to future instruction.

Your Example: Describe a time you reflected on a lesson that didn’t go as planned and what you changed next time.

4b: Maintaining Accurate Records

What principals look for: Organized grading systems, accurate attendance, and clear communication about student progress.

Your Example: Explain how you keep track of student work, attendance, or progress notes in an organized way.

4c: Communicating with Families

What principals look for: Positive, proactive, and culturally responsive communication with families.

Your Example: Share how you use newsletters, ClassDojo, or conferences to build strong partnerships with families.

4d: Participating in a Professional Community

What principals look for: Collaboration with colleagues, contributions to team goals, and support for school initiatives.

Your Example: Talk about a committee, PLC, or grade-level team you’ve been part of and how you’ve contributed.

4e: Growing and Developing Professionally

What principals look for: Engagement in professional learning, openness to feedback, and commitment to continuous growth.

Your Example: Include a PD session, workshop, or course that impacted your teaching practice.

4f: Showing Professionalism

What principals look for: Integrity, ethical behavior, advocacy for students, and sound judgment.

Your Example: Describe a time you went above and beyond for a student or demonstrated professionalism in a challenging situation.
