Domain 3: Instruction

This domain focuses on how teachers actively engage students in learning, use questioning and discussion effectively, assess understanding, and respond to student needs in real time. Principals look for lessons that are purposeful, interactive, and responsive.

3a: Communicating with Students

What principals look for: Clear directions, learning goals shared with students, and explanations suited to the learners’ age and level.

Your Example: Describe how you use “I can” statements, visuals, or modeling to clarify learning expectations for your students.

3b: Using Questioning and Discussion Techniques

What principals look for: Open-ended questions, equitable participation, and student-led discussion.

Your Example: Explain how you use wait time, turn-and-talks, or small-group discussions to promote deeper thinking.

3c: Engaging Students in Learning

What principals look for: Tasks that are challenging, relevant, and aligned to objectives; all students actively participating.

Your Example: Share an example of a hands-on, collaborative, or differentiated activity that engaged all learners.

3d: Using Assessment in Instruction

What principals look for: Ongoing checks for understanding; feedback that guides student improvement; students involved in self-assessment.

Your Example: Describe how you use exit tickets, thumbs up/down, or conferencing to gauge understanding and adjust instruction.

3e: Demonstrating Flexibility and Responsiveness

What principals look for: Adjusting instruction as needed; addressing misconceptions; persistence in helping all students succeed.

Your Example: Include a time you adapted a lesson based on student needs or reteaching for better understanding.
