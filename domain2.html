Domain 2: The Classroom Environment

This domain focuses on how teachers build a positive learning culture, establish respect, and manage classroom routines effectively. Principals look for evidence of strong relationships, smooth transitions, and consistent expectations.

2a: Creating an Environment of Respect and Rapport

What principals look for: Positive teacher-student and student-student interactions; tone that promotes trust and respect.

Your Example: Describe how you build strong, respectful relationships with your students or use positive language during lessons.

2b: Establishing a Culture for Learning

What principals look for: Students show pride in their work, persist through challenges, and value learning. Teacher expectations are high and clearly communicated.

Your Example: Describe how you encourage growth mindset or celebrate student effort and achievement.

2c: Managing Classroom Procedures

What principals look for: Smooth transitions, clear routines, and minimal loss of instructional time.

Your Example: Share how you manage transitions (morning routines, centers, dismissal) efficiently.

2d: Managing Student Behavior

What principals look for: Clear expectations, consistent consequences, and respectful redirection strategies.

Your Example: Include how you use positive reinforcement or restorative practices to maintain a supportive learning environment.

2e: Organizing Physical Space

What principals look for: Classroom setup that promotes engagement, accessibility, and safety for all students.

Your Example: Explain how your classroom arrangement supports collaboration or independent learning.
